# Major Question

> &ldquo;What is the most Ideal Keyboard for Human Efficiency and Ergonomics?&rdquo;

This question is a far more important question than it may seem at first glance. Currently (*almost*) all computers are using a keyboard layout known as &ldquo;**QWERTY**&rdquo;, which consists of having the previously mentioned letters at the top left section of the keyboard. To most of us humans, QWERTY seems like the default method of typing and we rarely even consider the thought that there would be any other alternatives. But as humans begin to transition more into a technological based society with keyboards being our main and almost constant method of input to computers; it is important to reflect on the keyboard which we are so used to. QWERTY was initially devised in 1867 for the use of the early typewriters. Not to discredit the wonderful inventions made by the creators, but the amount of science in relation to ergonomics and finger kinematics was severely lacking in comparison to today. If our keyboards have remained how they were since time long ago, that means that we as a society have not progressed from that point in time.

We are aware that is not the case, modern science has evolved tremendously since the 1800&rsquo;s and I believe that our keyboards should too. As people begin spending over 8-9 hours on keyboards, it is important to consider the practicality of this worldwide layout. It is not the most ergonomic layout possible, and is by far from being the most efficient layout possible as the creation of QWERTY was a method for typers to slow down while typing on typewriters as the arms would get jammed together. Therefore, as society progresses further into the realm of technology, it is imperative that the keyboard evolves in a similar manner; hence my proposal for the complete redesign of our current keyboard while keeping **ergonomics** and **efficiency** in mind.
